Atlanta, GA

During the month of October, Between the Two of These spans an exterior 80ft wall at the Digital Arts Entertainment Lab as part of the Window Project.

Real-time Interaction

The installation employs infrared cameras to sense motion on location. Movement from the street causes fragmentation and destruction of the projected scene.

Statement

of Space & Memory

Between The Two of These creates an active parallel between two present spaces. At night, the installation senses movements of passers-by in the physical space surrounding the projected screen and triggers the slow deconstruction of its virtual scene over time. This motion causes the fragmentation of a closeby object, and the inevitable total annihilation of the scene. Once both objects have obliterated their particles exchange and the space is fully restored. This constant de/reconstruction of virtual hands and heads is an entry point to themes of the temporality of memory and the poetic duality of virtual and physical space.

    Brannon Dorsey and Pablo Gnecco
